What You Can Do To Help The Environment

Use Reusable Items

Worldwide, as many as one trillion plastic bags are used each year.

There are more than eight million tons of plastic bottles in the sea each year.

A 2015 study estimated more than fifteen trillion pieces of plastic are in the ocean every year.

It is estimated that in the year 2050 there will be more plastic than fish in the sea.

Use reusable items to save the sea animals.

Start A Garden

Gardens help with reducing air and noise pollution, erosion, minimizing carbon footprint, and filtering groundwater.

Gardens also provide a nice food and home source for many kinds of animals and insects.

Starting a garden is a simple task that can create many positive changes for everyone around you.

You may also save money by growing some of your food yourself!

It can also help to make your yard look a little nicer!

Throw Trash Away Responsibly

Six pack rings float and are light enough to be easily moved by the wind.

Many animals mistake the floating objects as food and eat them.

Some young animals get trapped and as they grow the rings cut into their flesh.

Thousands of birds, turtles, marine mammals, and other wildlife are killed every year by discarded six pack rings.

Throw away your trash appropriately so that animals don't have to suffer.
